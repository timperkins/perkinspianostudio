Repertoire

Students learn classical repertoire through method books, collection series, and individual assignments. In addition, jazz music, lead sheet improvisation, popular and film music are taught and encouraged.

Technique

Proper technique is obtained by a combination of scales, arpeggios, block chords, and exercises such as Hanon and Pischna. Sight reading, music theory, composition, and ear training are also vital to the developing musician.

Performance

Pianists gain performance experience through monthly group classes and participation in studio recitals. Competitions such as MTNA, Encore, UMTA, and various concerto competitions are highly encouraged.

Musicianship

All students are expected to prepare their pieces to an appropriate level of musicianship. Tone quality, phrasing, voicing, articulation, and dynamics help to create an artistic interpretation of great music.

Adult Piano Lessons

Ever thought about getting back into piano or learning a new instrument? Adult lessons are a fun, flexible, and fulfilling way to engage with music and continual learning. Adults are welcome to enroll in private lessons. Alternatively, you can sign up for group adult lessons, which costs less per individual. The curriculum is very flexible and adaptable to the goals of the adult student.

  • Review of basic skills
  • Light Technique
  • Adult method books
  • Classical piano hits
  • Hymns/Popular music
